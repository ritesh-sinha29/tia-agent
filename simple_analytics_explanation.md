# Understanding the 6 Analytics Graphs

This document explains what each of the 6 analytics graphs does, why they are needed, how they work, and their roles in simple terms.

---

## 1. Focus Score (The Hero Panel)
* **What it is:** A single main number from **0 to 100** shown inside a circular gauge.
* **Why we need it:** To give you a quick "health check" on your productivity, like a credit score.
* **How it works:** 
  * It combines three metrics: completion rate (40%), on-time rate (40%), and high-priority accuracy (20%).
  * It shows if your score went up or down compared to last week.
  * Below the score, an AI text line explains exactly what caused the score to change.
* **Role:** The **main anchor**. It instantly alerts you if your overall productivity is getting better or worse.

## 2. Completion Velocity
* **What it is:** A filled line graph (Area Chart) showing tasks completed each day.
* **Why we need it:** To see if your daily output is steady or if you are working in erratic bursts.
* **How it works:**
  * Shows the last 14 days on the bottom axis and task counts on the left axis.
  * It overlays a smooth 7-day average line to show the general direction of your productivity.
  * A badge shows if you did better or worse than the previous week.
* **Role:** The **momentum tracker**. It helps you see which days of the week you get the most work done.

## 3. Priority Accuracy
* **What it is:** A bar chart combined with a line chart.
* **Why we need it:** To show if you are finishing your most important tasks on time, or only finishing easy, low-priority tasks.
* **How it works:**
  * Groups your high and medium-priority tasks week-by-week.
  * Vertical bars are split into two colors: dark for on-time completions, and light for late completions.
  * A line runs across the bars showing the overall percentage of on-time tasks.
* **Role:** The **prioritization check**. It helps you realize if you are procrastinating on important deadlines.

## 4. Task Status Distribution
* **What it is:** A circular ring chart (Radial Bar Chart) showing a breakdown of tasks by status.
* **Why we need it:** To show you exactly where your tasks are getting stuck (e.g., in progress, on hold, delayed, or not started).
* **How it works:**
  * Breaks down all tasks into five statuses: Not Started, In Progress, On Hold, Delayed, and Completed.
  * Represents each status as a colorful circular ring.
  * The center of the circle highlights the biggest problem area.
* **Role:** The **bottleneck detector**. It tells you exactly where your workflow is stalling.

## 5. Overdue Debt Tracker
* **What it is:** A stacked area graph showing new tasks vs. overdue tasks.
* **Why we need it:** To make procrastination visible. It warns you when old, unfinished tasks are piling up.
* **How it works:**
  * Tracks tasks week-by-week for the last 8 weeks.
  * It stacks new tasks on top of overdue tasks carried over from previous weeks.
  * If the overdue area grows too big, it draws a dashed line labeled **"Debt Zone"**.
* **Role:** The **burnout warning**. It reminds you to finish old work before starting new projects.

## 6. Deadline Pressure Map
* **What it is:** A bar chart showing deadlines per day.
* **Why we need it:** To help you see busy days coming up so you can plan ahead.
* **How it works:**
  * Displays the next 30 days.
  * Shows a bar for the number of tasks due on each day.
  * Automatically highlights the single busiest day of the month in a distinct color.
* **Role:** The **calendar planner**. It helps you spot deadline pile-ups so you can move tasks around early.
